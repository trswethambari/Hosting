Death


Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, at the age of 83. His death was a profound loss for India and the world, marking the end of an era for a leader who inspired millions through his vision, humility, and dedication to education and public service.

Circumstances of His Death

  • Location:
    Dr. Kalam was delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong when he collapsed on stage. He was in the middle of sharing his thoughts on "Creating a Livable Planet Earth" with students when he suffered a cardiac arrest.

  • Emergency Response:
    Despite immediate medical attention and being rushed to the nearby hospital, he was pronounced dead shortly after arrival. His sudden passing shocked many, especially given his active engagement with students and the public until his last moments.

Reactions and Tributes

  • National Mourning:
    Dr. Kalam's death was met with an outpouring of grief across the nation. The Indian government declared a seven-day mourning period, and flags were flown at half-mast in his honor.

  • Tributes from Leaders:
    Political leaders, scientists, educators, and citizens paid tribute to him. Prime Minister Narendra Modi described him as a "great scientist, a remarkable President, and a source of inspiration for millions." Many leaders expressed their condolences and reflected on his contributions to science, education, and nation-building.

  • Global Recognition:
    Dr. Kalam's influence extended beyond India, and many global leaders and organizations recognized his contributions. Condolences poured in from around the world, celebrating his vision and dedication to humanity.

Legacy and Commemoration

  • Educational Initiatives:
    Following his death, several initiatives were launched in his memory to promote education and innovation among youth. Institutions and universities held special events to honor his legacy, and scholarships were established in his name.

  • Books and Publications:
    His writings continue to inspire future generations. Many of his books, speeches, and quotes are studied and referenced in educational institutions, encouraging students to pursue their dreams and contribute to society.

  • Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Memorial:
    A memorial has been established in his honor in Rameswaram, his birthplace, where people can pay their respects and learn about his life and contributions.

Final Resting Place

Dr. Kalam was laid to rest at Pei Karumbu ground in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. His burial site is visited by thousands of admirers who come to pay their respects to a man who dedicated his life to the service of the nation and the upliftment of humanity.
